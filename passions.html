Some thoughts and one question.
We have seen an increase of "anti hate" movements. First, I commend the activists. Second, I find it very hard to be an active activist for such movements. Is this a personal flaw? Anti-hate is intrinsically obvious to me and for all people, things, and situations. I am not writing this to say "hey look at me," but to get to its potential source. My Mother had three things my sister and I could not say growing up: 'I hate,' 'stupid,' and 'shut up.' Is it that simple? Probably not, but I know that had a large impact on me. I've always thought that everyone knows something you don't or in other words, everyone has insight or something to teach to everyone else. In any case, I think the concept of 'hate,' is one on its path to extinction. Very optimistic.
Question: What happens when technology is able to create completely new forms of life?
I don't want to taint the readers own answering of this question, but I will say- this will one day be possible. Maybe not in my lifetime but relatively soon.
